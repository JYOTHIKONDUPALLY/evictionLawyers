What is Section 21 & Section 8 Eviction?

Section 21 (No-Fault Eviction)

Section 21 lets landlords reclaim property without proving tenant fault, for ASTs only.

When to Use Section 21:
Use when you want possession without tenant fault.
  • End of tenancy – Not renewing a fixed term
  • Periodic tenancy – Regain possession anytime
  • No fault needed – For compliant ASTs
  • Proper paperwork – Deposit & documents must be correct
  • 2-month notice – Legally required period

Section 8 (Fault-Based Eviction)

Section 8 applies when tenants breach the tenancy.Used for tenancy breaches like arrears, damage, or misconduct.

When to Use Section 8:
Use when tenants breach the contract
  • Rent arrears – Act before 8 weeks overdue
  • Property damage – Record and act quickly
  • Antisocial behaviour – Verified complaints
  • Unauthorized subletting – Breach of terms
  • Criminal activity – Connected to tenancy

Step-by-Step Eviction Process

When a tenant fails to vacate the property after being served with a valid notice, the landlord must take the next step by applying to the court for possession. This formal process ensures you recover your property lawfully and in line with the Housing Act. The court will guide the matter through a series of stages, typically taking 6–12 weeks.

1
Notice Service

Serve a valid Section 21 notice, ensuring compliance with all legal prerequisites and providing the mandatory 2-month notice period.

2
Tenant Response Period

Allow time for the tenant to vacate voluntarily during the notice period. The process concludes if the tenant leaves by the end of this time

3
Post-Notice Review

If the tenant remains in occupation after the notice expires, review compliance with notice requirements and confirm readiness to proceed with court action..

4
Court Application

File an accelerated possession claim with the court to regain possession.

5
Possession Order

The court grants a possession order—often without a hearing if the claim is uncontested.

6
Bailiff Enforcement

If the tenant still does not vacate, apply for a bailiff warrant and enforce possession as necessary.
